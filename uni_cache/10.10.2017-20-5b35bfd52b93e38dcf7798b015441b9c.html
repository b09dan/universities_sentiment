Studying at Lancaster University

Lancaster University is home to more than 12,000 students. In The Guardian University Guide Lancaster came 7th and was ranked as 12th in The Times.

LUSU, Lancaster University\'s Students\' Union is the social hub on campus, with venues on each site. Throughout the year it provides events, gigs and theme nights including markets, a campus festival and celebration events. The Sugarhouse is the main venue. It has two rooms that host DJs and bands and is situated in the centre of Lancaster. It\'s only open to students and is the biggest club in the city.

There are plenty of societies and sports clubs available, from the 20 minute and glee society to fencing and football clubs. Volunteering is widely encouraged and can help individuals build confidence and gain skills at the same time as making new friends and benefitting the local community. For those who want to get involved in helping on environmental projects then GreenLancaster offers the chance to do so around Lancaster and back on the campus.

The brand new Sports Centre costing £20 million, has both indoor and outdoor facilities that include a swimming pool, climbing wall, sauna and steam room, gym and fitness centre, three rugby pitches, six football pitches, eight tennis courts, five netball courts and a Crown Bowling Green.

The Nuffield Centre is among the largest theatres in Europe and hosts some of the best talent in music, dance and theatre. It works in conjunction with several programmes at the university and is part of the university\'s offering to the arts alongside the Great Hall which holds many classical concerts and the Peter Scott Gallery which is a free gallery exhibiting many well known works from artists in recent years.



The main Bailrigg campus is virtually self-contained with many places to eat, cafés, bars, two banks a post office, newsagents, charity shop, bakery and an ice cream parlour. There is also a pharmacy, dentist and doctors on campus plus acres of green space with views of the mountains and coastline.

The City of Lancaster is full of history and charm with a vibrant music scene in its local pubs and venues. There is a huge range of places to eat, drink and shop plus plenty of acts to see at the arts centre and theatre. Recent shows have included Michael McIntyre, Eddie Izzard and Lee Evans.
