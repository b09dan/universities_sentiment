Top universities in the world featured in the ShanghaiRanking ARWU league table for 2017

\n
\n \n \n \n
\n August 15 2017
\n\n \n

\n

Harvard University has maintained the top spot in the ShanghaiRanking\xc2\xa0Academic Ranking of World Universities 2017\xc2\xa0for the 15th year running.\xc2\xa0

\n

In fact, the top 10 universities remain unchanged from last year, although there has been some shifting in positions. The University of Cambridge, for example, has overtaken the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and the University of California, Berkeley to take third position.\xc2\xa0

\n

THE World University Rankings 2018 coming soon: what you need to know

\n

Eight of the universities in the top 10 are from the US (Harvard University \xe2\x80\x93 first; Stanford University \xe2\x80\x93 second; MIT \xe2\x80\x93 fourth; Berkeley \xe2\x80\x93 fifth; Princeton University \xe2\x80\x93 sixth; Columbia University \xe2\x80\x93 eighth; the California Institute of Technology \xe2\x80\x93 ninth; and the University of Chicago \xe2\x80\x93 10th) alongside two UK universities (Cambridge \xe2\x80\x93 third; and the University of Oxford \xe2\x80\x93 seventh).\xc2\xa0

\n

The University of Tokyo\xc2\xa0is ranked in 24th place and is still the highest ranked university in Asia.\xc2\xa0Tsinghua University (48th) enters the top 50 for the first time and becomes the third top-ranked Asian university behind Kyoto University (35th). The University of Melbourne (39th) in Australia tops the universities in Oceania.

\n

ShanghaiRanking Global Ranking of Academic Subjects 2017: results

\n

Since 2003, ShanghaiRanking has been producing a league table of the top 500 universities in the world. For the first time this year, the consultancy has also published the list of universities ranked between 501 and 800 as the\xc2\xa0ARWU World Top 500 Candidates.\xc2\xa0These universities have the potential to break into the Top 500 list in the near future. The US and China are the two most represented countries of the Top 500 candidates, both with 55 institutions each.

\n

Top five universities in the 2017 ShanghaiRanking Academic Ranking of World Universities

\n

1. Harvard University\xc2\xa0

\n

Harvard University is the oldest university in the US, having been formed in 1636, and is considered one of the most prestigious in the world.

\n

The museums affiliated with the institution hold more than 28 million works of art, artefacts, specimens, materials and instruments.\xc2\xa0

\n

Harvard\xe2\x80\x99s alumni include\xc2\xa0eight US presidents, several foreign heads of state and 62 living\xc2\xa0billionaires. Some 130 Nobel laureates,\xc2\xa018 Fields Medallists, and\xc2\xa013 Turing Award winners\xc2\xa0have been affiliated\xc2\xa0with the university as students, faculty or staff.

\n

2. Stanford University\xc2\xa0

\n

Stanford University is known for nurturing an entrepreneurial\xc2\xa0spirit among its students, a legacy of its founders Jane and Leland Stanford. It is also renowned for its relationship to Silicon Valley.\xc2\xa0

\n

The 8,000-acre campus is located in California\xe2\x80\x99s Bay\xc2\xa0Area. There are more than 625 student associations at the university, including the student newspaper, The Stanford Daily.

\n

3. University of Cambridge

\n

The University of Cambridge is the only UK university to feature in the top five. Affiliates of the University of Cambridge have won some 90 Nobel prizes, and\xc2\xa0Nobel prizes have been won by Cambridge affiliates in every category.

\n

The list of high-achieving alumni from the institution is endless, but prominent among them are Stephen Hawking, David Attenborough, Virginia Woolf and Zadie Smith.

\n

4. Massachusetts Institute of Technology

\n

This specialist technology and science institution invented the first computer in Cambridge, Massachusetts in 1928. The Whirlwind I is still on display at the MIT Museum.

\n

Some 25,800\xc2\xa0active companies have been started or co-founded by living MIT alumni, showing just how much impact MIT has had on innovation.\xc2\xa0

\n

5. University of California, Berkeley

\n

Rounding off the top five is the University of California, Berkeley. The university is not without its unusual traditions, and\xc2\xa0students are expected to roll down 4.0 Hill on campus to get good grades. However, if you step on the Berkeley seal nearby, it has been said that you will be cursed for the year. \xc2\xa0

\n

The tradition obviously works as Berkeley faculty, alumni, and researchers include\xc2\xa091 Nobel laureates. The institution\xe2\x80\x99s alumni have also\xc2\xa0achieved\xc2\xa013 Fields Medals,\xc2\xa023 Turing Awards, 45\xc2\xa0MacArthur Fellowships,\xc2\xa020\xc2\xa0Academy Awards, 14\xc2\xa0Pulitzer Prizes\xc2\xa0and\xc2\xa0117 Olympic gold medals.\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0
