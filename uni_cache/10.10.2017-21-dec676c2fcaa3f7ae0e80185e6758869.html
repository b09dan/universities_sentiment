Studying at University of Bath

Bath is known for excellence in teaching and research, a superb student experience, and providing outstanding preparation for the workplace. Bath is a top five UK university (Guardian University Guide 2018) and ranked Gold in the Teaching Excellence Framework.

The Claverton Down campus is located two kilometres from Bath City Centre and has undergone extensive building and refurbishment over the past few years to include new and updated research facilities, teaching rooms, halls of residence and a new Student Centre where information and advice can be found for wellbeing, visas and immigration, disabilities and funding. The Parade connects most of the learning facilities on campus and is home to cafés, bars, banks, a union shop, two supermarkets and restaurants.

TeamBath is the universities sports team brand and has many successes behind its name making it within the top five universities in the UK for sport. The University of Bath owns one of the greatest sports facilities in the UK and comprises of 4 squash courts, an indoor and outdoor athletics track, lacrosse, hockey, football, american football and rugby pitches, indoor and outdoor tennis courts, basketball, badminton and netball courts, a 50m swimming pool, a fitness suite, a rowing shed, gyms and karate and jitsu centres. The university facilities have helped many athletes achieve world standards in sport including Amy WIlliams (Gold Vancouver 2010), Ben Rushgrove (Silver Beijing 2008), plus 31 athletes who competed in the London 2012 Olympics and Paralympics, 7 of which won medals.

>br>
All programmes at the university have placement opportunities many of which can be abroad using the links added to and maintained by the university over the years on the Erasmus and other programmes across the globe. 60% of students at The University of Bath do a work placement as part of their studies.

The Students\' Union provides more than 100 clubs and societies and the bar \'Plug\' and nightclub \'The Tub\' are constantly hosting bands, comedy and club nights. Located in the new Student Centre you will also find the student newspaper, ‘Bath Impact’, television station ‘Campus Television’ and University Radio Bath alongside the Enterprise Office that will encourage, support and advise students with an entrepreneurial spirit who want to start their own business. If studying, partying and playing sport doesn\'t take up the majority of your time then students\' can also get involved in The Volunteer Centre or find a job through Joblink, the employment centre on campus.

Apart from the fields surrounding the main university campus, it has to be up there with one of the ugliest campuses in the UK. If students weren't to see the league tables and choose based on looks, I guarantee a large amount of students wouldn't apply. The main "Parade" is gloomy and wind tunnel-esque and when you look around you see big concrete blocks which you'll have the pleasure of studying in for the next three (or four) years. The single library isn't big enough to accommodate the students which need to use it and there is no where else to really do work unless you want to work in one of the noisy campus eateries, which are overpriced and have generally poor quality food (however I do rate the beef chilli, just the right amount of spice and good ratio of meat to sauce.) The Students Union is small and offers very little in terms of somewhere to go with your mates and you'll find yourself bored in there in ten minutes or so.
