Where do the world\xe2\x80\x99s top CEOs go to university?

\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n

Here\xe2\x80\x99s where you should go to college if you want to be big in the business world

\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n January 18 2017
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n
  • \n \n By Alice Durrans\n
    • \n
\n \n \n \n \n \n \n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n

Choosing a university is far from easy \xe2\x80\x93 with so many places to go and courses to study, picking the right one is a minefield. But what if you based your decision according to where the CEOs of the world\xe2\x80\x99s most successful companies spent their college days?

\n

Times Higher Education has found out which universities were home to the top business men and women of 2017. The Alma Mater Index 2017 (\xe2\x80\x9calma mater\xe2\x80\x9d being Latin for \xe2\x80\x9cnourishing mother\xe2\x80\x9d) ranks universities according to how many qualifications they have awarded to chief executives of companies that feature in Fortune magazine\xe2\x80\x99s Fortune Global 500. This list is a compilation of the world\xe2\x80\x99s 500 largest companies by revenue, whose earnings in 2015 totalled $27.6 trillion (\xc2\xa322.3 trillion).

\n

From the survey, it appears that having a degree from a world-leading university is the way to go for a stronger chance of a career in a business powerhouse. It also seems that domestically educated chief executives are preferred at the helm of an organisation: almost two-thirds of the companies on the Fortune Global 500 list are run by chief executives who have a degree from the country where the company is based.

\n

Information on the education of chief executives was researched using biographies, company websites, press releases, industry association websites, conference speaker biographies and news sources. Where a chief executive has qualifications from more than one university (including undergraduate and master\xe2\x80\x99s degrees, doctorates and other postgraduate qualifications) the companies\xe2\x80\x99 revenues are attributed to each university that has awarded their chief executive a degree.

\n

The index is limited by the public availability of information, and it was not able to find details on all individuals\xe2\x80\x99 educational history. Nonetheless, the results give an insight into the academic background of the business elite.

\n

Top five universities that have educated the most global business leaders

\n

Harvard University

\n

Harvard Business School in Massachusetts enrolled 936 students on its two-year MBA course in 2016. It has an impressive research department and catalogue of published work, including Why They Do It: Inside the Mind of the White-Collar Criminal, written by Professor Eugene Soltes.

\n

Harvard has awarded 29 qualifications to 26 chief executives of companies with a combined 2015 revenue of more than $1.4 trillion.

\n

Stanford University

\n

In 2015, Stanford had its 125th anniversary. Stanford\xe2\x80\x99s Graduate School of Business offers a two-year MBA programme and a one-year MSx management programme along with several other course options.

\n

Notable alumni in business include the founders of Google (Sergey Brin and Larry Page), the head of Netflix (Reed Hastings) and the co-founder of Nike (Phil Knight).

\n

Stanford awarded 14 degrees to 12 chief executives of companies worth a total of $729 billion.

\n

HEC Paris

\n

HEC Paris is a business school that claims in its mission statement to be \xe2\x80\x9ctraining the global leaders of tomorrow\xe2\x80\x9d.

\n

Focused on nurturing close connections to the corporate world, HEC Paris has an impressive alumni network including the CEOs of L\xe2\x80\x99Or\xc3\xa9al, EDF and Allianz France. It offers a master\xe2\x80\x99s in management, an MBA and a summer school for recent graduates looking to sharpen their business acumen.

\n

HEC Paris awarded 12 degrees to 12 chief executives running companies with total revenues of $516 billion.

\n

\xc3\x89cole Polytechnique

\n

\xc3\x89cole Polytechnique is a science-focused institution, boasting one of the world\xe2\x80\x99s best engineering schools. Renowned for its 20-laboratory-strong research centre, it says that innovation in the sciences and economics is the key to its success.

\n

The university educated 11 chief executives and awarded 11 degrees, but their companies generated more revenue than Stanford\xe2\x80\x99s alumni, with a total 2015 revenue of more than $840 billion.

\n

Cornell University

\n

Situated in New York, Cornell University and its College of Arts and Sciences offers a wide range of undergraduate courses, ranging from medieval studies to game design.\xc2\xa0

\n

Cornell awarded 10 degrees to seven chief executives, whose companies had a total revenue of $547 billion.
