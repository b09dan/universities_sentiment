Studying at University of East Anglia

The University of East Anglia is classed in the top 20 in the UK and has been listed in the top ten for student satisfaction in the National Student Survey for several years running. Situated in Norwich, Norfolk, the university is made up of 15,000 students from over 100 countries.

The on campus, largest indoor sports facility in the UK, Sportspark has a lot to offer students including a swimming pool, badminton, archery, yoga, table tennis, football, hockey, cricket, fencing, athletics track, climbing wall and gym. There are also floodlit outdoor pitches and courts, a gymnastics centre, and 40 acres of playing fields.

Norwich is a short fifteen minute walk from campus and only two hours away from London. It has everything that you could want or need from a university city; shopping, history and architecture, markets, pubs, clubs and cinemas. For those that want to party on campus the LCR venue run by the Union of UEA Students hosts regular events such as films, gigs and fashion shows. Visiting artists include Ed Sheeran, Kaiser Chiefs, Coldplay and Professor Green. The Union of UEA Students also has over 200 clubs and societies available plus several advice and listening services.

The library is situated in the heart of campus and houses 800,000 books, 1,200 study areas, and 260 computers. Open nearly all hours of the day and night, it offers places to study, printing facilities and WiFi. Also on campus is the world famous Sainsbury Centre for Visual Arts, a free museum with special exhibitions and work by well known artists plus workshops, talks and places to sit and relax and get a coffee in the Modern Life Café.



The Employability Strategy at the University of East Anglia means that the most is made of students time whilst studying to prepare them for the world after graduating. Many opportunities are given for individuals to improve their skills in presenting, communication and team working through tailored courses and a chance for many to carry out a work placement locally or abroad. After graduation the university also continues to give help to former students in their chosen careers and increase their chances of employability.

Well known people that have studied at UEA include Radio 1 DJ Greg James, Booker Prize winning author Anne Enright, founder of Café Rouge Karen Jones, comedian Paul Whitehouse, Actors Matt Smith and John Rhys-Davies plus Members of Parliament Judith Chaplin, Jon Owen Jones and Douglas Carswell.
