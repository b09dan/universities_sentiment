Best universities in Europe 2018

\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n

Times Higher Education World University Rankings data reveal the top colleges in Europe

\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n September 14 2017
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n

European universities take just over 40 per cent of the places in the list of the best universities in the world.

\n

The\xc2\xa0Times Higher Education\xc2\xa0World University Rankings 2018\xc2\xa0rank 1,000 universities around the world and include 1,102 in the overall list. Just over 400 of them are universities in Europe.

\n

A UK university - The University of Oxford - takes the top spot in our Europe ranking, while 92 other UK universities appear in the league table.\xc2\xa0Germany is the next most-represented European country, with 44 institutions, many of them within the top 100.

\n

Of the universities in mainland Europe, a Swiss institution \xe2\x80\x93\xc2\xa0ETH Zurich \xe2\x80\x93 Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich \xe2\x80\x93 achieves the highest rank, making it into the top five in Europe and the top 10 in the THE World University Rankings 2018.

\n

The top 10 European universities include UK, Swiss, German and Swedish institutions, but UK universities take most of the top spots.

\n

\n

Institutions were assessed on their teaching environment, research environment, citations (research influence), industry income and international outlook, using the World University Rankings methodology. The full methodology can be viewed here.\xc2\xa0

\n

While Italy boasts 39 universities in the ranking, its top-ranked institution \xe2\x80\x93\xc2\xa0Scuola Superiore Sant\xe2\x80\x99Anna\xc2\xa0in joint 74th place \xe2\x80\x93 doesn\'t make it into the top 50 in Europe or\xc2\xa0the top 100 in the World University Rankings 2018.

\n

Elsewhere in Europe, Scandinavian countries perform well, with Sweden and Finland punching above their weight relative to their population size with 11 and nine institutions, respectively, in the ranking. Sweden\xe2\x80\x99s top university \xe2\x80\x93 the\xc2\xa0Karolinska Institute\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 is in the top 40 of the World University Rankings 2018, while Finland\xe2\x80\x99s\xc2\xa0University of Helsinki\xc2\xa0makes the top 100. The Netherlands also performs very strongly.

\n

In the Europe ranking, other strong performers relative to their population size include Denmark (seven universities \xe2\x80\x93 led by\xc2\xa0Aarhus University) and the Republic of Ireland (nine universities in total).

\n

Overall, 35 European countries are represented in the Europe 2017 ranking, including Spain, Russia, Greece and Serbia.\xc2\xa0

\n

Top 5 universities in Europe

\n

1. University of Oxford

\n

There is no specific date to mark the beginning of the University of Oxford but it is thought that it was established some time in the 13th century. Since then, it has become one of the most\xc2\xa0prestigious universities in the world. However, women were not admitted to the university until 1878, and it wasn\'t until 1920 that women were awarded degrees. The last of the all-male colleges began to accept women in 1974.

\n

The university has the largest library system in the UK with more than 11 million volumes. There are also a number of museums and galleries including the Ashmolean, which is the oldest museum in the UK and the oldest university museum in the world. It holds significant collections and works from artists such as Michelangelo, Turner and Picasso.\xc2\xa0

\n

2. University of Cambridge

\n

The University of Cambridge is a public research university in the UK, established in the 13th century. \xc2\xa0

\n

The university, like many other well-established institutions, has many traditions. Until 1909, a metre-long wooden spoon was presented to the\xc2\xa0student with the lowest passing honours grade in the final examinations of the mathematical tripos (one of the courses available at the university). The spoon is now on display in St John\xe2\x80\x99s College.\xc2\xa0

\n

3. Imperial College London

\n

Imperial College London is the only university in the UK to focus entirely on science, engineering, medicine and business.\xc2\xa0

\n

The university has aimed to strengthen its ties with the rest of Europe by investing more than \xe2\x82\xac15 million (\xc2\xa313 million) in new grants for researchers in all four faculties, launching a European\xc2\xa0Partners Fund\xc2\xa0to help\xc2\xa0develop new collaborations\xc2\xa0with European colleagues and working with the\xc2\xa0EU Commissioner for Research, Science and Innovation\xc2\xa0to discuss ways of strengthening European research ties.

\n

The university attracts students from more than 125 countries every year and is considered one of the most international universities in the world.\xc2\xa0

\n

4. ETH Zurich\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich

\n

ETH Zurich is a science, technology, engineering and mathematics university. The institute has produced more than 20 Nobel prizewinners, including Albert Einstein.\xc2\xa0

\n

About 35 per cent of the institution\xe2\x80\x99s students are from abroad and the university has established an International Knowledge Base to forge research and teaching relationships across the world.

\n

5. University College London (UCL)

\n

UCL was the first university to be established in London and the first in the UK to be entirely secular and to admit students regardless of their religious beliefs.\xc2\xa0

\n

It has been a member of the\xc2\xa0League of European Research Universities\xc2\xa0since January 2006 and is currently one of five British members (the others are the universities of Cambridge, Edinburgh, Oxford and Imperial College London).

\n

The university has its main campus in central London, with a number of facilities across the city and a satellite campus in Doha, Qatar.\xc2\xa0
