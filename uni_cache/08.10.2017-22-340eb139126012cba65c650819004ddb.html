Studying at University of Sheffield

The University of Sheffield in South Yorkshire is a leading research university. It has received consistently good rankings from the Good University Guide, regularly achieving a place in the twenty best-scoring universities in the United Kingdom. Over 24,000 students study in the five faculties within the city: the Faculty of Medicine, Dentistry and Health, the Faculty of Pure Science, the Faculty of Arts and Humanities, the Faculty of Social Studies and the Faculty of Engineering. In addition it has an International Faculty in City College, Thessaloniki in Greece which provides an excellent opportunity to study abroad. As such Sheffield University attracts a large number of international students every year

The university is not completely campus based, instead having a collection of buildings located quite close to each other, in one instance covering a mile-long stretch of road on the west side of Sheffield city centre. The smaller St George’s campus lies east of here and contains several faculties including engineering and computer science.

The Student Union is the centre for student services and activities throughout the university. As well as the general shops and a bank, the Union contains a Student Advice Centre providing support and a sympathetic ear to any student in difficulties. There are also several food and drink outlets. After stopping at the Copy Shop to get your essay printed, or the Jobshop for some advice on part time work in the city or arranging a placement, you can head over to Bar One to have a drink with your mates and watch the sports on its big screen TVs.

Students are offered a lot of extracurricular activities. More than 250 societies meet around the campus and city, including Creative Writing, War Games, Knitting, faith-based groups, and a large Sports Committee. Besides this there are RAG (‘Raising and Giving’ events throughout the year to encourage students to raise money for charitable causes. Past events have included a treasure hunt, bungee jump and night hike. Students in Sheffield will also enjoy the relatively low cost of living, compared to other major UK cities.



Sheffield University is keen to prepare its students for life beyond graduation, and maintains working partnerships with several high profile companies throughout the UK, with its largest graduate employer being the National Health Service. It and also operate a placement system, links to graduate schemes, and further training in management. It has 84 specialised research institutions as well as the departmental research facilities.

The university regularly scores over 90% in the National Student Survey for student satisfaction across its faculties, with 91% of students going on to employment or further study within six months of receiving their degrees and 68.5% of these being graduate level positions. Sheffield attributes this to the emphasis it places on understanding and meeting student needs both during and after their time at the university.
