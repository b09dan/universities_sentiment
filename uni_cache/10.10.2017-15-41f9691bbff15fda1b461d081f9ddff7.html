Academy Awards nominations 2016: Where did the Oscar nominees go to university?

\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n

The film professionals nominated for Academy Awards for movies they have directed, produced, acted in, written and edited are a mixed bunch: these are the universities in the running to win the alumni Oscars.

\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n February 26 2016
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n
  • \n \n By Carly Minsky\n
    • \n
\n \n \n \n \n \n \n
\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n \n
\n

This year\xe2\x80\x99s Oscars \xe2\x80\x93 the 88th\xc2\xa0Academy Awards \xe2\x80\x93 are upon us, and more than 200 individuals are nominated for films that they have directed, produced, written, edited or performed in.

\n

Where did the 2016 Oscar nominees get the education and training that would set them on the path to success? All over the place, as it turns out.

\n

While many of the big names didn\xe2\x80\x99t pursue higher education, or dropped out without completing their studies, there are more than 100 universities around the world that can claim at least one alumnus among the nominations.

\n

Unsurprisingly, universities in North America and the UK dominate the table, but Sweden, Australia, France, the Republic of Ireland, Mexico, South Africa and Japan also make appearances.

\n

Out of all the nominations associated with a university, more than 13 per cent are associated with a university in California, suggesting that many stars are home-grown near Hollywood.

\n

But before you budding film-makers pack your bags for the Golden State, it\xe2\x80\x99s worth considering that this may not be a direct result of helpful networking opportunities; it\xe2\x80\x99s possible that through living in the area, Californian-educated graduates are simply more likely to consider the film industry as a career path.

\n

Without further ado, these are the universities up for Academy Awards:

\n

\n

New York University: 9 nominated alumni

\n

Winning again this year, New York University leads the way with nine alumni nominations \xe2\x80\x93 the same number as last year, although none of the graduates in the running this year were on last year\xe2\x80\x99s shortlist.

\n

The nominees include producer Steve Golin, who graduated from NYU\xe2\x80\x99s Tisch School of the Arts in 1976, and actress Rooney Mara, who attended NYU\xe2\x80\x99s Gallatin School of Individualized Study to study psychology, international social policy and non-profits. She graduated just six years ago and has received numerous award nominations since then.

Harvard University: 7 nominated alumni

\n

As one of the best universities in the world, it\xe2\x80\x99s no surprise that Harvard\xe2\x80\x99s graduates go on to achieve greatness. The Harvard alumni are also a well-rounded bunch, landing nominations for producing, acting, writing, documentaries, music and cinematography.

\n

Matt Damon, nominated for his role in The Martian, kick-started his career while studying at Harvard, performing in student plays and writing the screenplay for Good Will Hunting, which was nominated for nine Academy Awards in 1998.

Central Saint Martins, University of the Arts London: 5 nominated alumni

\n

As a world-leading centre for art and design, it\xe2\x80\x99s no surprise that the graduates of Central Saint Martins in London, UK are over-represented in behind-the-scenes aspects of the film industry.

\n

British costume designers Sandy Powell and Jenny Beavan have scooped up numerous awards for their work on high-grossing films, and are nominated this year for work on\xc2\xa0Carol\xc2\xa0and\xc2\xa0Mad Max: Fury Road.

\n

Michael Fassbender, nominated for actor in a leading role for his portrayal of Steve Jobs, and Tom Hardy, up for actor in a supporting role for The Revenant, both attended Central Saint Martins\' Drama Centre London.

\n

Joshua Oppenheimer, the American director nominated for his documentary this year, achieved his PhD from Central Saint Martins after studying at Harvard University.

Yale University: 4 nominated alumni

\n

All of Yale\xe2\x80\x99s nominees have also attended another university in the United States, making these alumni some of the most educated in Hollywood. Spotlight\xe2\x80\x99s director and writer attended Boston College and Harvard University, respectively, while composer Thomas Newman, who wrote the score for Bridge of Spies, studied composition at the University of Southern California before transferring to Yale.

\n

Adam Stockhausen received a Master of Fine Arts from Yale School of Drama in 1999. He has previously won an Academy Award for his set design for Wes Anderson\xe2\x80\x99s The Grand Budapest Hotel.

Stanford University: 3 nominated alumni

\n

Better known for producing billionaire entrepreneurs, Stanford University has seen significant success in the film industry, but its alumni are the more unusual characters in Hollywood.

\n

This year, Pakistani journalist and documentary film-maker Sharmeen Obaid-Chinoy, a Stanford alumna, is nominated for her short film about honour killing in her own country.

\n

The composer David Lang attended Stanford as an undergraduate, and it was there that he met a replacement teacher from the University of Iowa called Martin Jenni, whom he credits for setting him on his illustrious career path.

\n

Pixar producer Nicole Grindle, nominated for a short animation, studied for a master\xe2\x80\x99s in documentary production at Stanford, graduating in 1987.

The Best of the Rest

\n

In total, 59 universities included in the World University Rankings 2016 received at least one Oscar nomination for a graduate or a student who had once enrolled at the institution. A further 52 non-ranked universities \xe2\x80\x93 mostly specialised institutions that do not qualify for the rankings \xe2\x80\x93 also stack up a nomination or two.

\n

There\xe2\x80\x99s clearly no one specific path to success in Hollywood, as the list shows. Unusual talent is found and nurtured all over the world.
