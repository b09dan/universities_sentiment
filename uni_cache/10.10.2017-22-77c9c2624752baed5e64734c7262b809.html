“I went to UBC for my first yearQuite BrightPhilosophy
I went to UBC for my first year of University and was not at all pleased. I am transferring this fall and am very happy about it.

As others have said, you are just a number. You get lost in your classes. I had friends who didn\'t go to a single class EVER and took the finals and passed. No one notices if you are there or not. Classes had upwards 300 people in big lectures. The course work was somewhat challenging, not too bad though.

There are two kinds of people I met at UBC, either people who lock them selves in their room studying 24/7, or people who never go to class and don\'t care about school at all. I met alot of unmotivated people, nice and friendly people. If you like smoking weed, go to UBC, actually just move to Vancouver and it will save you a few grand.

I was an international student and I got RIPPED off SSOOOOO bad. I paid about $22,000 for 1 year of university. I\'ve come to learn that you can get an education like this in a state school and pay the fraction of the price.

I lived in the dorms, which was basically like living in a trash dump. People were constantly vomitting, peeing, throwing food, spilling beer... There was a constant smell or rancid meat everywhere... not pleasant. Canada\'s largest nudist beach is a 5 minutes walk from the dorms which was pretty funny. Lots of naked hippies selling drugs.

The best thing about UBC was Vancouver. The city is awesome, so much to do, alot of things to get distracted with though, especially since the drinking age is 19. If you are an international student I DO NOT recommend you go to UBC, you will pay WAY too much money for a school that is not worth it.
