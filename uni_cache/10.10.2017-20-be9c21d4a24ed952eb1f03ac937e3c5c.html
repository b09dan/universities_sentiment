Studying at University of Dundee

The University of Dundee is well known for its contribution to Law in the UK and is the only university to allow students to qualify in both Scottish and English law. The university is ranked within the top 200 in the world, 1st for student experience in the UK and is in the top ten in the UK for both learning and teaching. Students that graduate from the University of Dundee have some of the highest earning potential for starting salaries across the UK, coming second only to Oxbridge.

Equally as well known as Law at the University of Dundee is the medical and dental school which was named as best in the UK by the Guardian in both 2008 and 2009 for the impact of the work and research carried out here.

All degrees have options for internships and modules that help to plan a career. Students come from over 85 countries to study at Dundee, similarly the opportunity is given in reverse for students from the UK who can make use of links with institutions overseas and benefit from taking part in an exchange programme in countries such as Australia, Hong Kong, and North America which in turn give students great life experience and a set of enviable skills gained from living, working or studying abroad.



The student experience is enhanced by the fact that the cost of living is well below the national average. Also contributing highly to the experience is Dundee University Students\' Association. Located in the centre of campus, DUSA hosts many different events throughout the academic year including carpark raves, a pool competition and regular features such as SKINT every Tuesday, Sin City on Fridays and Distorted every other Friday. In a recent Which? guide DUSA came top out of all universities in the UK for its student nightlife, social scene and range of student offerings throughout the year. As well as venues and pubs there are several food outlets, a student shop, a Student Representative Council and hundreds of sports clubs and societies covering all sorts; Juggling, charity, music, film, big band and performance to name a few.

Over recent years the university has had millions invested into it\'s award-winning facilities. Everything students need is available on campus but the City of Dundee is close by and offers plenty to do including golf, sailing, galleries, a theatre. music halls, shopping centres, clubs and pubs.

I love Dundee uni! It's a small enough place that you can get to know a lot of people very well and everyone is friendly and welcoming, however it's still a lot bigger than home and it allows to you to embrace city living. My course is excellent and the lecturers are great.
