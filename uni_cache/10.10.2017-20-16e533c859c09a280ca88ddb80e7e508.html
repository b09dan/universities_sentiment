Studying at University of Liverpool

Based just five minutes walk away from Liverpool City Centre is the University of Liverpool. It consists of three faculties; Humanities and Social Sciences, Science and Engineering and Health and Life Sciences, and was listed as 28th in the Times Good University Guide in 2011. The majority of studies and student life is carried out on its main campus with the exception of veterinary science which is carried out on the Leahurst campus and is globally recognised as an animal medicine centre of expertise. Both sites have undergone significant investment in development and modernisation in recent years. There are more than 400 programmes available to more than 27,000 students that include representatives from more than 100 countries.

The University of Liverpool is in the top 24 research-led universities in the UK and is well known for its pioneering work. Since its foundation in 1881 there has been nine winners of the Nobel Prize at the University of Liverpool, including work with malaria by Sir Ronald Ross in 1908, Professor Charles Barkla’s discovery of the properties of X-rays in 1917, and winner of the Nobel Peace Prize 1995, Professor Joseph Rotblat for his work with nuclear disarmament.

Whatever your interests there is a club, society or team here to help you explore them further. Students that take part in other activities outside of their degree can gain many skills to be added to their CV and will enhance their prospects of employment after graduation. Courses and societies are available in everything from art, music and languages to learning leadership skills, and computing. Also available to many students is the opportunity to take part in volunteering, work placements and overseas study.



Teaching at the university is often delivered by internationally renowned staff and makes use of the various on-campus facilities including two libraries and The Active Learning Laboratory, a new £35m development that helps students to gain \'industry ready\' skills directly linking to one of the universities aims of helping students to graduate as employable individuals.

The Guild of Students is the hub of social life at the university. Within the building students can access the Athletics Union, meeting rooms, bars, cafés plus live music, comedy and theatre in one of its four main venues. Sporting life here is also very active and many of the teams under Sport Liverpool are at a premier level. Opportunities are available for all levels of sporting ability and sports include men and women\'s football, Rugby Union, Hockey, Netball and Basketball.
