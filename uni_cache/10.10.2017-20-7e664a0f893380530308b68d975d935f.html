Studying at University of Nottingham

The University of Nottingham dates back to 1881 and is claimed as one of the oldest higher education institutions in Britain. It currently offers around 50 courses in it\'s five faculties of Art, Engineering, Medicine and Health, Science and Social Sciences.

In total Nottingham has 7 award-winning sites, five of which are located in the historic city of Nottingham plus an additional two in China and Malaysia that were introduced as part of a growth strategy. Since its creation the Malaysia campus has won awards for Enterprise and Industry.
The University of Nottingham is highly regarded in its research in the world of health and has won two Nobel Prizes confirming its world class ranking in this area. In the UK it was ranked as 7th in terms of it’s research power in 2010.
The facilities offered at The University of Nottingham include it\'s own Arts Centre which regularly hosts comedy, dance, theatre and exhibitions. Each hall of residence has it\'s own bar and common room and several have their own sports facilities. When its time to head into Nottingham for the evening students can head to the Students\' Union Box Office to find out what\'s happening and buy tickets although Students\' are just as likely to have a good time on campus at the Students\' Union with it\'s range of bars, shops and activities.
For sporty students the Athletic Union provides an opportunity for everyone to join in whether  you are at the top level or just fancy getting fit. To add to it\'s array of awards, The University of Nottingham\'s alumni has won 11 olympic medals over the past 20 years.
The National Student Survey and other rankings prove that the student experience and quality of education at The University of Nottingham is high. Students are encouraged to shape their own learning by using a mixture of resources from traditional learning in taught classes to using the extensive online resources available from this leading research institute and taking advantage of the visual technologies such as video conferencing for nurses, lab visualisers for vets and simulated chemical plants for those in engineering.
There are numerous opportunities for students to reach their potential and have a lot of fun at the same time including studying abroad, giving yourself the extra edge above other graduates by studying for The Nottingham Advantage Award, or becoming a student ambassador for one of the most environmentally friendly universities.
